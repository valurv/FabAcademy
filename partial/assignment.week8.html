Week 8 computer-controlled machining

I was very excited about this week assignment so I ended up making two furnitures, one shelf and one coffee table. I started with the shelf. First I drew a 1/2 dome (or 1/4 bubble) in SketchUp and used the plug in Slice modeller to make it as press fit. I imported that drawing into Vcarve. Then I added T-bones so that it would fit nicely together. I made it out of 12 mm mdf, because some day I will paint it. I used a router table to make 5 mm radius on every outside edge just to make it look better. The painting will wait for another day, but I'm pretty happy with the shelf as it is.

I got a new sofa in my living room so the old coffee table didn't fit. So I did some measuring and thought about what I wanted in a table. I needed drawers to hide the remote and stuff and I also wanted a big drawer for the keyboard and mouse, but there is a computer connected to the TV screen. With that in mind, I designed the table and drew it in SketchUp. Again I used 12 mm mdf, because I knew I would definitely paint the table and I wanted to have the finishing surface smooth.

I was happy that the whole table fitted easily to one board of 12 mm mdf but I used two 30 mm mdf glued together to make the table legs. I wanted the table legs to have some decoration/old look so I used turning lathe to get that look.

I started by making toolpath with 1/4 down cut two flute endmill 3 mm down around every piece, to make the top surface cuts better so the milling bits would not tear up mdf. Then I switched to up cut two flute endmill and finished all the cutting with it, except I made tabs to hold every piece down but I made little bigger tabs to hold the drawers in place. The reason for that to make it easier for assembly. Before I put the table together I made some decoration with router to get that old fashion look I wanted on the table. Then I put the table together with stable gun and wood glue, and when the glued was dried I sawed the drawer tabs away so I could take them out before the paint job.

To get that old fashion look I painted the table with chalk paint so I could get some "weathering" on the edges, and I used a pallet wood for the table top to perfect the old rough look. Needless to say I'm very happy with the table, and so are the rest of my family.

Still I needed some handles on the drawers so I drew them also in SketchUp and 3D milled it in the Shopbot. You can see some great tutorial about 3D milling on www.shopbottools.com.

I'm positive I will put the handles on the drawers in the near future.
