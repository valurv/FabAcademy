Week 10 molding and casting

This week assignment is to design a 3D mold, machine it and cast parts from it. I have been working on a Daft Punk helmet for fun that I will someday mold so I decided to make a ear piece for the helmet for this week assignment.

The ear piece has to be in two parts because in between there is supposed to be led light. I drawed the piece in Skecthup and saved it as an stl file. Then I opened the file in Partworks3D and made paths there.

I decided to use 1/8 ball nose milling bit and I used the same milling bits both for refining and finishing. Because then I didn´t have to chance bit in the middle of the project and the z zero would be the same. I milled it in a machine able wax in the Shopbot.

Do not try to clean the milling bit with your fingers, it's sharp!

I screwed down wooden pieces in to the sacrificial layer all around the wax. I couldn’t move the wax piece so I didn’t need to put any pressure on the top of the wax. I wanted to collect the leftovers of the wax so I didn't turn the vaccume on, because you can melt back to some block and use it again. Then the machine worked it‘s magic.

I wanted to try the OOMOO 25 on a test subject before I used it on my mold because I suspected that it was too old to use, and sad to say, I was right.

Because the wait would be far to long for new OOMOO 25 I used Silicon Rubber instead. I measured the volume of the silicon rubber by pouring water into the mold and devided the water into two cups. I marked each cup by drawing a line on it and used the cups as measurement to know how much compound A and B I should use. Then I blended the compound together in a bigger cup and mixed it by stirring it together with back and forward movement, because circular movement adds more air bubbles in the silicon rubber.

I poured the compound into the machinable wax mold and let it dry over night. When the negative mold was ready I mixed Hydro stone compound with pure Icelandic water and poured that into the mold. I tapped the mold a little bit to make the air bubbles come up and waited for it to dry. When Hydro stone compound had dried I took it out of the silicon mold and my two pieces for the ear piece was ready. When I will finish the ear piece in the future I will make a acrylic circle that will fit between the two pieces and there will be led in the ear piece that will glow through the acrylic.
